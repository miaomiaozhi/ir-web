讲座摘要：
One of the fundamental questions in self-assembly is to unveil the noncovalent interactions between several distinct building blocks, which consequently leads to the control of self-assembly into desired nanostructures with tailored functionalities. Self-assembly of nanoparticles into periodic ordered structures, called superlattices or supracrystals, has generated much attention and is crucially important for the fabrication of advanced devices for electronic and optoelectronic applications. The current strategy for integrative self-assembly relies on elaborate design of two building blocks encoded with specific information such as coordination geometry and directionality, giving rise to programmed integrative assemblies in proper stoichiometric ratios. Nevertheless, developing an effective strategy towards the ordered assembly of two distinct components is challenging, because the incompatible interaction presents from one to another. We explore and demonstrate the extension of the concept of integrative self-assembly to mesoscale supracrystalline structures, in which inorganic nanocrystals and organic molecules are held together through noncovalent interactions.
